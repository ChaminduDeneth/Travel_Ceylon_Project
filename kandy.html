TRAVEL CEYLON

Kandy

Temple of the Tooth

The Temple of the Tooth is one of the most sacred Buddhist shrines in the world, believed to house the relic of the tooth of the Buddha. Located in the heart of Kandy, this UNESCO World Heritage site is a must-visit for anyone interested in Sri Lankan culture and history. Visitors can tour the temple and witness daily rituals and ceremonies, including the traditional drumming and chanting that takes place several times a day.

Royal Botanical Gardens

Just a short drive from Kandy in the town of Peradeniya, the Royal Botanical Gardens are a stunning display of Sri Lanka's rich flora and fauna. With over 4,000 species of plants, including orchids, palms, and giant bamboo, the gardens are a feast for the senses. Visitors can also see a collection of spices and medicinal plants, as well as a stunning avenue of royal palms.

Kandy Lake

Kandy Lake is a man-made lake that was built in 1807 by the last king of Kandy. Situated in the heart of the city, it's a popular spot for locals and tourists alike. Visitors can take a leisurely walk around the lake and enjoy views of the city and surrounding hills, as well as the many birds that call the lake home.

Bahirawakanda Temple

Bahirawakanda Temple is known for its giant Buddha statue, which stands at 88 feet tall and can be seen from many parts of the city. Located on a hillside just outside of Kandy, the temple offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding mountains. Visitors can climb the steps to the top of the temple for panoramic views and to see the intricate murals that line the walls of the temple.

Udawatta Kele Sanctuary

Just a short hike from the city center, Udawatta Kele Sanctuary is a peaceful nature reserve that's home to a variety of flora and fauna, including monkeys, birds, and butterflies. The reserve is also home to several historic sites, including an ancient temple and a colonial-era cemetery. Visitors can hike the trails and enjoy the peaceful surroundings, as well as the stunning views of Kandy from the hilltop.

Kandy View Point

Located on top of a hill on the outskirts of the city, Kandy View Point offers breathtaking panoramic views of Kandy and the surrounding mountains. Visitors can take a tuk-tuk or hike to the top for stunning views, as well as to see the nearby tea plantations and waterfalls. The view point is also a popular spot for sunset views, and many visitors come to watch the city light up as the sun goes down.
