Anuradhapura

Jaya Sri Maha Bodhiya

The Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi is sacred fig tree located in Anuradhapura. It is believed to have been grown from a sapling of the original Bodhi tree, and is also a popular pilgrimage destination. The site is particularly beautiful at night when the tree is lit up with thousands of lights.

Ruwanwelisaya

Ruwanwelisaya is a large stupa located in Anuradhapura, which is considered one of the most important stupas in Sri Lanka. It was built by King Dutugemunu in the 2nd century BC and is known for its impressive architecture and intricate carvings. Visitors can climb to the top of the stupa for panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Kuttam Pokuna

Kuttam Pokuna, also known as the Twin Ponds, is a unique attraction located in Anuradhapura. It is a pair of large stone pools, which were used for bathing by the monks who lived in the nearby monasteries. The pools are known for their impressive architecture and intricate carvings.

Abhayagiri Vihara

Abhayagiri Vihara is a historic monastery located in Anuradhapura, which was once one of the largest monasteries in the world. It was built in the 2nd century BC and is known for its impressive architecture and intricate carvings. Visitors can explore the ruins of the monastery and learn about its rich history.

Samadhi Statue

Samadhi Pilimaya is a small but significant Buddhist temple located in Anuradhapura. It is known for its unique architecture and is believed to have been built during the reign of King Devanampiya Tissa in the 3rd century BC. The temple is considered an important pilgrimage site and is visited by many Buddhist devotees.

Isurumuniya

Isurumuniya is an ancient temple located in Anuradhapura, which is known for its impressive rock carvings. The temple dates back to the 3rd century BC and is home to many important Buddhist relics. Visitors can explore the temple and admire the intricate carvings on the rocks
