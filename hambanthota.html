Hambanthota

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is the most popular and second-largest national park in Sri Lanka. It is located in the Hambantota district and is known for its diverse wildlife, including leopards, elephants, and crocodiles. Visitors can take a safari tour to explore the park and its many natural wonders.

Mulkirigala Rock Temple

The Mulkirigala Rock Temple is a historic temple located in the Hambantota district. It is built into a giant rock and features a series of caves with stunning Buddhist murals and statues. Visitors can climb to the top of the rock for panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Bundala National Park

Bundala National Park is another popular national park located in the Hambantota district. It is known for its diverse birdlife, including many migratory species. The park is also home to elephants, crocodiles, and other wildlife, and offers a variety of safari tours for visitors.

Tangalle Beach

Tangalle Beach is a beautiful beach located in the Hambantota district. It is known for its soft golden sand, clear waters, and palm trees. The beach is a popular destination for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing. Visitors can also explore the nearby fishing village and sample fresh seafood.

Kataragama

Kataragama is a pilgrimage town located in the Hambantota district. It is considered one of the most sacred places in Sri Lanka and is home to many important Hindu and Buddhist temples. Visitors can also attend the famous Kataragama Festival, which takes place annually in July or August.

Ridiyagama Safari Park

Ridiyagama Safari Park is a wildlife park located in the Hambantota district of Sri Lanka. It covers an area of over 500 acres and is home to a diverse range of animals, including elephants, leopards, crocodiles, and many species of birds. The park was established in 2016 with the aim of promoting wildlife conservation and eco-tourism in the region.
