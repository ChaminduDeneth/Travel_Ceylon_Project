Galle

Unawatuna

Unawatuna is a coastal town located in southern Sri Lanka, known for its beautiful beach and coral reef. It is a popular destination for diving and snorkeling, and visitors can also explore the nearby Japanese Peace Pagoda and Yatagala Raja Maha Viharaya.

Galle Dutch Fort

The Galle Dutch Fort is a historic fort located in the city of Galle, which was built by the Dutch in the 17th century. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its impressive architecture and historical significance. Visitors can explore the fort and its many attractions, including the lighthouse, clock tower, and numerous museums.

Hikkuduwa Beach

Hikkaduwa Beach is a popular beach located in the town of Hikkaduwa, which is known for its clear blue waters and vibrant coral reef. It is a popular destination for diving and snorkeling, and visitors can also explore the nearby turtle hatchery and Buddhist temple.

National Museum Galle

The National Museum Galle is a museum located in the city of Galle, which showcases the history and culture of the southern region of Sri Lanka. The museum is housed in a historic building and features exhibits on archaeology, art, and natural history.

Roomassala

Rumassala is a scenic hill located near the town of Unawatuna, which is known for its stunning views and rich biodiversity. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill and explore the surrounding jungle, which is home to many rare plant and animal species and visitors can also explore the nearby Japanese Peace Pagoda

Dutch Reformed Church

The Dutch Reformed Church is a historic church located in the city of Galle, which was built by the Dutch in the 18th century. It is known for its impressive architecture and is a popular tourist attraction. Visitors can explore the church and learn about its rich history.
