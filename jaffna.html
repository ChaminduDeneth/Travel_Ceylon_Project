TRAVEL CEYLON

Jaffna

Jaffna Fort

The Jaffna Fort is a historical fortress built by the Portuguese in the 17th century. It is located in the heart of Jaffna city and is a popular tourist destination. The fort was also used as a military base during the Sri Lankan Civil War.

Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil

The Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil is a Hindu temple located in Nallur, Jaffna. It is one of the most important Hindu temples in Sri Lanka and is dedicated to Lord Murugan. The temple is known for its colorful annual festival which takes place in August.

Jaffna Public Library

The Jaffna Public Library is a historic library located in Jaffna city. It was established in 1933 and was one of the biggest and most important libraries in Sri Lanka. The library was burned down in 1981 but has since been rebuilt and is now a symbol of Jaffna's cultural identity.

Keerimalai Hot Springs

The Keerimalai Hot Springs is a natural spring located in Keerimalai, Jaffna. The water in the springs is believed to have healing properties and is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. The site also features a historic Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Delft Island

Delft Island, also known as Neduntheevu, is a small island located off the coast of Jaffna. It is known for its wild ponies and unique landscapes, including the Baobab trees which were brought to the island by Arab traders. The island is accessible by ferry from Jaffna city.

Point Pedro Lighthouse

The Point Pedro Lighthouse is a historic lighthouse located in Point Pedro, Jaffna. It was built by the British in 1916 and is one of the tallest lighthouses in South Asia. The lighthouse offers stunning views of the Jaffna coastline and is a popular spot for tourists to visit.
